Places worth visiting

The Fairy Pools

The Fairy pools are a beautiful and unique natural attraction on Skye.
The stunning aqua blue pools are easily accessible and afford the opportunity to enjoy the stunning Cuillin Hills scenery.

The Quiraing

The Quiraing is a stunning and unique landscape located in the north of the Isle of Skye.
It's part of the Trotternish ridge that was formed due to a massive landslip that created high cliffs, hidden plateaus, and striking rock formations.

The Dunvegan Castle and Gardens

Any visit to the Isle of Skye is incomplete without visiting the Dunvegan Castle and Gardens.
Visitors can enjoy tours of the oldest continuously inhabited castle in Scotland, delight in the beauty of its gardens or stay in one of its charming Victorian cottages.

The Neist Point Lighthouse

Neist Point is one of the most famous lighthouses in Scotland.
From the path you will see stunning views of the high cliffs and the lighthouse itself, the view becoming even more spectacular at sunset.

The Fairy Glen

Hidden in the hills of Uig, the enchanting geographical formations that form the Fairy Glen has gained this special miniature landscape on Skye a reputation for being a place of myth and wonder.

The Cuillin Mountains

The Cuillin is a world famous mountain range on the Isle of Skye, with steep cliffs and deep cut corries and gullies.
At the heart of The Cuillin lies one of Britain's most famous corrie's, Loch Coruisk, a deep and forbidding body of water.

The Old Man of Storr

The Old Man of Storr is an iconic rock pinnacle in the Trotternish region.
This geological marvel attracts countless visitors year-round who trek uphill for breathtaking, panoramic views of the island and the Atlantic Ocean.
